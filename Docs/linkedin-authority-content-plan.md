Primewayz helps UK SMEs improve, connect and support the digital systems their businesses depend on.

We work across five practical service areas:

1. Website visibility and conversion — making websites easier to find, trust and enquire from.
2. CRM and workflow automation — connecting enquiries, records, follow-up and reporting.
3. Software and product engineering — building and improving applications through structured delivery.
4. Managed application and website support — keeping live systems stable while they improve.
5. Remote IT team extension — adding dependable technical capacity with clear ownership.

Our focus is delivery ownership, not generic digital transformation language. We help businesses clarify what is broken, what should be improved first, and how work should continue without losing continuity.

Services describe the business problem. Engagement models describe how the work is organised — for example a foundation sprint, fixed-scope project, monthly development capacity, managed support or dedicated remote capacity.

## D. Leadership About section

I work with UK SMEs that need practical technical ownership across websites, CRM workflows, software platforms and live applications.

Much of the useful work is not greenfield. It is improving inherited systems, connecting disconnected workflows, clarifying priorities and keeping delivery moving without destabilising operations.

My focus is product and delivery ownership across business, UX and engineering: understanding what already exists, deciding what should change next, and building enough continuity that progress does not depend on undocumented individual knowledge.

I care about honest claim discipline. Public case studies should show real delivery responsibility without inventing metrics, overstating ownership or exposing confidential client identity.

If you are reviewing website visibility, CRM workflows, software delivery, managed support or remote technical capacity, I am happy to discuss the practical next step.

### Post 4 — The difference between a service and an engagement model

**Opening hook**
Buying “software development” is not the same as choosing how the work should be organised.

**Body**
A service answers the business problem: website visibility, CRM workflows, product engineering, managed support or remote capacity.

An engagement model answers the delivery shape: foundation sprint, fixed-scope project, monthly capacity, maintenance mode or dedicated team extension.

Confusing the two creates mismatched expectations. Monthly development capacity is one engagement model for continuous software work — not the whole company category.
